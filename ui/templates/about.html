About Beautiful Trouble

Beautiful Trouble is a book, web toolbox and international network of artist-activist trainers whose mission is to make grassroots movements more creative and more effective. Used by campaigns and in classrooms across the world, it’s an invaluable resource for veterans and newbie activists alike, pulling together a toolbox of interlocking design principles to instigate everything from a flash mob to a revolution.

About Beautiful Solutions

Beautiful Solutions gathers the most promising and contagious strategies for building new institutions grounded in justice and sustainability. It is designed as a companion to Beautiful Trouble, and builds off its popular model for distilling the component pieces of effective organizing. In addition to the web platform, a book version, Beautiful Solutions: A Toolbox for the Future is scheduled for Fall 2015.

Core Team

Rachel Plattus

Rachel Plattus is Co-Editor of Beautiful Solutions and Co-Director of Organizing at the New Economy Coalition (NEC). She coordinates NEC's youth and student organizing and community reinvestment efforts, and works to build broad community, movement and organizational engagement in creating an alternative economic system that is restorative to people, place and the planet. Someday she would like to be a heron or a whale. She lives in Jamaica Plain, MA.

Eli Feghali

Eli Feghali is Co-Editor of Beautiful Solutions and Director of Communications and Online Organizing for the New Economy Coalition (NEC). He is a Lebanese-American who spent two months in 2011 occupying a park in Boston to prove another world was possible. Through NEC and Beautiful Solutions, Eli works to tell the story of what that other world could look like -- and how we can get there. Despite his love for pizza, he is vegan.

Dave Oswald Mitchell

Dave Oswald Mitchell is Editorial Director of the Beautiful Trouble project. He is a freelance editor, researcher, and communications strategist whose work focuses on supporting social change and community organizing initiatives. He edited Briarpatch Magazine from 2005 to 2010. His interests include found haiku, photography, and going elsewhere.

Andrew Boyd

Andrew Boyd is Co-Editor and Wrangler-in-Chief of the Beautiful Trouble project. A long-time veteran of creative campaigns for social change, he led the decade-long satirical media campaign “Billionaires for Bush” and co-founded the Other 98%. He's the author of three books: Daily Afflictions, Life’s Little Deconstruction Book and the creative action manual The Activist Cookbook. Unable to come up with with his own lifelong ambition, he’s been cribbing from Milan Kundera: “to unite the utmost seriousness of question with the utmost lightness of form.” You can find him at andrewboyd.com.
